If you read my [previous post](../00-turningpoint/) then you'll know that my first major step to becoming financially free was to educate myself. I needed to learn money. As strange as that sounds I never learned money. You probably didn't either. I can almost guarantee you weren't taught money in school. You were taught to work for money so you were taught skills, albeit, mostly useless skills depending on your current occupation. 

What you need to understand is you were not taught to make money. You were taught to work for it. I would rather live my life, not work it away in front of a computer screen. I'd rather not spend 40 hours a week in meetings I've been in 10 times discussing the same things. This drove me to where I am now.  

I needed to learn a skill that made money. Not a skill to use to work for money. With most things, you usually want to educate yourself before getting started. Day trading seemed to be the only skill that was purely money based. I ordered a few trading books and read them cover to cover. I practiced their strategies using paper trading accounts. I was pleased with the results but I felt like I still didn't know why the price was doing what it was doing and why. 

I decided to try some real trading and was profitable for a couple of days. Then greed kicked in. I took a pretty large loss. It hurt pretty bad. I learned my lesson though. I had taken the trade based on the strategies in the books I just didn't manage my risk and I overleveraged. This is a recipe for disaster when it comes to trading.

I take full responsibility for my loss. Losses always hurt but it wasn't something I couldn't recover from. Something still bothered me. Why did the price do what it did? There wasn't any news event or anything I was aware of that should've dropped the price as it did. As it turned out I was someone else's liquidity. 

Tiktok with its algorithm turned me on to [The Inner Circle Trader](https://www.theinnercircletrader.com/) (ICT). His content gave me the substance I was looking for. It is a highly accurate trading model and it explains why price does what it does. There are still wins and losses but the rules around the framework he provides can provide near-perfect entries on trades. I have taken trades that have returned 200-300% of what I put into it at precise points. I have exited those same trades with pinpoint accuracy on their reversals. The risk management strategies he presents are clear and precise. If you understand his strategies can control your emotions you can become a consistently profitable trader.  

I want to share some information about the trading platforms I use. I started with [Robinhood](https://join.robinhood.com/kalebh19). Robinhood offers a 1.5% APY on uninvested cash. If you pay for their gold membership they offer up to 4.65% APY on uninvested cash. Knowing that my IRA and savings weren't making half this, I pulled the savings from my bank and took the tax hit on the IRA and placed everything in Robinhood. 

I made more of the interest in a month than I had in the entirety of the savings account lifetime. I put very strict rules around that money now sitting in my trading broker. I could not use but a small percentage of it for trading. If for some reason I had a drawback I would continue to follow ICT's risk management strategies and cut my leverage in half. 

After a while of trying to analyze things on Robinhood and trade options to maximize profits, I'd miss entries trying to navigate the site or app. I was day trading and scalping sometimes to the minute. Robinhood's tools just didn't allow me enough flexibility for this.

Here comes Youtube's algorithm introducing [Webull](https://a.webull.com/KFgte9M9JAXue0sshX). Webull as it turns out offers 5.0% APY on any uninvested settled cash. There is no subscription required. I signed up, tested the waters and not long afterward moved a majority of my money into Webull. So if you have a savings account giving you anything less than 5.00% APY you may want to do your research. You might be surprised to know the 1.5%, 4.65%, and 5.00% APY is coming from the same banks you have in your savings account collecting 0.01% APY. 

I use both Webull and Robinhood. I use Robinhood for longer-term investments and Webull for day trading. Webull also acts as my savings account with a very nice 5.00% APY. I use Robinhood's cash account card for my spending money. Robinhood gives you a debit card you can put money on. When you spend money using the cash account card it will round up your purchase to the nearest dollar and deposit that into your Robinhood brokerage. For example, if you spend $3.30 using the cash card it will take $0.70 and put it in your Robinhood brokerage to be invested. Robinhood also offers bonuses on your round-ups so the more you use it the better.
